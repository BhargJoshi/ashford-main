The Language

How You Speak About Markets
Reveals How You Trade Them

The amateur's vocabulary is built on hope, prediction, and ego. The professional's is built on probability, process, and evidence. Same market. Same chart. Completely different lens.

The Amateur ⚡
◎ The Professional
On Entry "I think this is going to go up — it looks strong." Based on feeling, hope, or a hunch. No defined criteria.
vs
On Entry "My criteria are met. I'm entering at this level with this stop." Rule-based entry. No ambiguity. The system decides, not the mood.
Concept: Entry Decision
On Loss "The market just stopped me out to take my money." External blame. The market is seen as the enemy.
vs
On Loss "My stop was hit. That was the cost of being wrong this time." Full ownership. Losses are expenses — not failures of character.
Concept: Accepting Losses
On FOMO "I missed the move — I need to chase it before I miss more." Fear-driven. Enters late into stretched moves.
vs
On FOMO "That wasn't my setup. The next one will be — I wait." Patience as a competitive advantage. Opportunity is never scarce.
Concept: Fear of Missing Out
On Winning "I'm on fire today — let me size up and take more trades." Overconfidence after a streak. Risk management abandoned.
vs
On Winning "Good session. I'll review what worked and stick to my process." Detachment from results. The system is evaluated, not the ego.
Concept: Winning Streak Psychology
On Risk "I'm confident about this one — I'll put in a bigger size." Confidence is not a risk management strategy. It's a bias.
vs
On Risk "My risk is 1% of capital per trade — regardless of how I feel." Position size is a function of account math, not emotional conviction.
Concept: Position Sizing
On Prediction "I predicted this move perfectly — I knew it was going to happen." Hindsight bias. The market wasn't "read" — it was rationalized after.
vs
On Prediction "I identified a high-probability scenario and managed the trade with my plan." No prediction. Only probabilities and structured responses to what unfolds.
Concept: Forecasting vs Probability
